# MAFI Archive — Annotation Guidelines
**Agente Fílmico MAFI v1.5**

---

These guidelines define the content and scope expected for each annotation field in the MAFI spreadsheet. Please read them carefully before beginning. The annotations you produce will directly feed the retrieval system used by Agente Fílmico, so precision, consistency, and specificity matter more than length.

---

## Fields

### `description`

Describe what happens in the film, in sequence. Name the people, objects, and spaces present, and describe their actions and movements as they unfold within the frame. Write as if describing the film to someone who cannot see it.

Do not interpret the meaning or intent behind what is shown — that belongs in other fields. Focus on what is visible and audible: who is there, what they do, what surrounds them, and in what order.

---

### `historic_context`

Identify the historical event, social context, or period to which this film belongs. Name relevant institutions, political figures, social movements, or documented events that are visible or audible. Include approximate dates where known.

This field should be factual and specific, not interpretive. Its purpose is to anchor the film in verifiable history so the system can retrieve it in response to historical queries.

---

### `aesthetic_critical_commentary`

How do the filmmaker's formal choices shape the political, anthropological, or historical meaning of what is shown? Consider: frame composition, shot scale, camera angle, depth of field, the use of off-screen space (*fuera de campo*) and off-screen sound, the handling of focus (*fuera de foco*), duration, and stillness.

Focus on what the aesthetic decisions reveal or withhold — not merely on describing them. What does the choice of a fixed frame, or a particular shot distance, or the absence of intervention, make possible or foreclose in terms of meaning?

---

### `production_commentary`

Document any known production details: the filmmaker's biography or broader body of work, the conditions under which the film was made, any technical particularities (equipment, format, shooting context), or relevant editorial choices made in post-production.

If nothing is known, leave this field **blank**.

---

### `tags`

A comma-separated list of keywords, themes, proper names, object types, musical styles, fashion details, or any other element — large or small — that makes this film distinctive or searchable.

Do not limit yourself to the film's main subject: incidental details are equally valuable. A dog breed, a style of hat, a song title, a brand name, a type of tree — anything that might help a user find this film through an unexpected angle is worth including.

Use lowercase, singular nouns where possible (e.g. `quiltro`, `chupalla`, `cumbia`, `volcán`, `bandera chilena`).

---

### `annotator_comments`

Any observation that does not fit the fields above. This might include: curatorial intuitions about the film's value or its place within the collection, uncertainties about the identification of people, places, or sounds, alternative readings of what is shown, or notes on particular difficulties encountered during annotation.

This field is for your voice as an annotator. It will not be surfaced directly by the system but will be read by the project team.

---

## General Notes

- Annotations should be written in **Spanish**, following the language of the collection, unless instructed otherwise for a specific field.
- The `description` field will be the primary field used for semantic retrieval. Invest the most care here.
- If a film is incomplete, damaged, or unclear in ways that affect your annotation, note this in `annotator_comments`.
- When in doubt about the identification of a person, place, or event, annotate what you can observe directly and flag the uncertainty rather than guessing.
